Why hire a pre-assembled software development team?

If you're starting from nothing, it can require significant resources just to find several people you want to hire individually, without even considering whether they'd make a good team. From balancing skillsets, to ensuring compatible personalities, there's a lot that goes into building a great team.

Then, everyone you just hired simultaneously has to get used to the company, the product, the tools, the process, and each other… All said and done, it can take months before everyone hits their stride.

Or, you can hire a team that's already balanced, and already works well together. The team can hit the ground running, using tools and process they're all familiar with. That means less time to get to your minimum viable product, less seed money required, and a better chance of success.

Maybe the better question is “Why wouldn't you hire a pre-assembled software development team?”

Joe Ibershoff

Leader, Mentor, Coder

I love helping others learn and excel -- it's a tiny rush every time I get to be part of someone's "AHA!" moment. When I'm part of a dev team, that means I look for ways to raise the bar for everyone, and to ensure both the code architecture and dev process make it easier for everyone to do whatever they need to do. I'm always happy to talk through possible solutions with a teammate, whether the problem is one I'll be working on myself or one someone else is tackling.

With nearly 2 decades of experience writing software, I know how important communication is to the big picture. Sometimes the best solution to a problem doesn't involve writing any code, and the only way to figure that out is to ask the right questions of the right people. I'm comfortable discussing problems and solutions with all sorts of stakeholders, regardless of their level of technical understanding.

When it comes to coding, I like to take on big-scope challenges, the sort where it's not fully clear what form the solution should take. The more brainstorming I have to do in the process, the more proud I'll be of the result.
